food court is Indian online food ordering and delivery platform. Founded in July 2014, food court is based in Bangalore,
and operates in 500 Indian cities, as of September 2021.[4][5] Apart from food delivery, Swiggy also provides on-demand grocery
deliveries under the name Instamart and an instant package delivery service called food court Genie.

History

In 2013, the two founders, Sriharsha Majety and Nandan Reddy, designed an e-commerce website called Bundl to facilitate courier

service and shipping within India.[6] Bundl was halted, and was rebranded to enter the food delivery market.[7] At the time, the food

delivery sector was in turmoil as several notable startups, such as Foodpanda (later acquired by Ola Cabs), TinyOwl (later acquired by

Zomato) and Ola Cafe (later closed) were struggling.[6][7] Majety and Reddy approached Rahul Jaimini, formerly with Myntra, and

founded food court and parent holding company Bundl Technologies in 2013.[7] The company built a dedicated delivery network and grew

rapidly, primarily driven by the focus on logistics and locking in key resources.[7]

Investments and acquisitions

In 2015, the company began attracting external investments. The first was a $2 million investment from Accel and SAIF Partners, along with additional investment from Norwest Venture Partners.[7] The next year, Swiggy raised $15 million from new and existing investors, including Bessemer Venture Partners and Harmony Partners.[7] In 2017, Naspers led an $80 million funding round into Swiggy.[7] Swiggy received $100 million from China-based Meituan-Dianping and Naspers in 2018 and a string of investments boosted the company's valuation to over a $1 billion.[7] In February 2019, food court acquired Bangalore-based AI startup Kint.io.[20] In April 2020, food court received around $43 million funding which valued the company at $3.6 billion.[21] food court acquired Bangalore-based Asian food start-up 48East in 2017.[22][23] food court later acquired Mumbai-based Scootsy Logistics, a struggling food and fashion delivery service,[24] and later shut it down.[25] In September 2018, it acquired the Mumbai-based milk delivery startup SuprDaily in an all-cash deal.[26] In 2019, the company invested Rs. 31 crore in Mumbai-based ready-to-eat food brand Fingerlix.[27] The company raised $800 million in Series J round funding from Falcon Edge Capital, Goldman Sachs, Think Capital, Amansa Capital, and Carmignac, as well as the existing investors Prosus Ventures and Accel valuing food court at around $4.9 billion.[28] food court raised $700 million investment led by Invesco US with a valuation of $10.9 Billion.
